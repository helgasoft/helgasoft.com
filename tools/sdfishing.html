Charts - San Diego fishing by boat

Data for this study was collected from website sandiegofishreports.com.
It contains daily trip reports by San Diego recreational fishing vessels from January 2009 to end of June 2018.
Each report includes the starting date and duration of the trip, boat name, number of anglers and fish count by species.

The amount of data is considerable - 109 boats with 51,422 fishing trips during 9.5 years, with a catch of 89 ocean species - Albacore,Angel Shark,Bank Rockfish,Barracuda,Barred Sand BassBat Ray,Bigeye,Black Skipjack,Blacksmith,Blacksmith Perch,Blue Marlin,Blue Perch,Blue Shark,Bluefin,Bocaccio,Bonito,Brown Rockfish,Brown Smoothhound,Cabezon,Calico (Kelp Bass),Calico Rockfish,Chilipepper,Copper Rockfish,Corbina,Cowcod,Croaker,Dorado,Escolar,Finescale Triggerfish,Giant Seabass,Giant Squid,Gray Smoothhound Shark,Grouper,Halfmoon,Halfmoon Perch,Halibut,Hammerhead Shark,Jack (Spanish) Mackerel,Kelp Rockfish,King Salmon,Leopard Shark,Lingcod,Lizardfish,Lobster,Mackerel,Mako Shark,Octopus,Opah,Opaleye,Pargo,Perch,Petrale Sole,Pinback Shark,Red Rock Crab,Red Rockfish,Red Snapper,Rock Crab,Rock Sole,Rockfish,Rubberlip Seaperch,Sand Bass,Sand Sole,Sanddab,Sargo,Sculpin,Seven Gill Shark,Sheephead,Shortbill Spearfish,Shovelnose Guitarfish,Skipjack,Soupfin Shark,Spanish Jack,Spider Crab,Spotted Sand Bass,Striped Marlin,Swordfish,Thresher Shark,Tomcod,Treefish,Triggerfish,Vermilion Rockfish,Wahoo,White Croaker,White Seabass,Whitefish,Whitespotted Greenling,Yellowfin,Yellowfin Croaker,Yellowtail,Yellowtail Rockfish.

Data preparation and analysis was mostly done through R library dplyr. Charts were generated with R library ggplot2 and library formattable was used for the two tables.
